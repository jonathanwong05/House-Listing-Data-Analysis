Final Takeaways

Overall Findings

Our comprehensive analysis of the US house listings dataset for 2023 reveals a complex picture of the national real estate market. By combining multiple visualizations, we have identified both clear regional variations and surprising insights into which factors drive property values.

The interactive house map confirmed that listings cluster in high-demand areas, consistent with market trends. Our scatter plot comparing lot area to listed price revealed little correlation, indicating land size alone doesn't strongly determine price. The choropleth map of price ratios showed interesting regional patterns where some states like Wyoming and Colorado have listing prices significantly exceeding market estimates (ratios of ~1.08), while states like New York and Wisconsin show the opposite trend with listing prices below market estimates (ratios of ~0.96). Many states, including California, maintain ratios close to 1.0, suggesting alignment between listing prices and market valuations despite their high absolute prices. The D3 barplot showed California and Massachusetts having the highest average prices, while Midwestern states like Mississippi, Ohio, Illinois, and Indiana had much lower averages. Our analysis of amenities (bedrooms and bathrooms) versus price indicated that while property features contribute to value, they don't solely determine it.

Implications and Future Work

These findings suggest a complex interplay between location, market dynamics, and property features. High-priced markets in states like California and Massachusetts are driven by strong local economies, high urban demand, and luxury market outliers. The more modest pricing in many Midwestern states reflects different economic conditions and housing availability. The weak correlation between physical property attributes and listing prices emphasizes that neighborhood quality, education access, job opportunities, and urban proximity are major price determinants.

For homebuyers and real estate professionals, these findings highlight the importance of local market conditions and regional economic health when making property decisions. Consumers might choose high-priced markets for their robust economies and amenities despite the premium cost, while others may seek more affordable options in the Midwest. Expanding to multi-year data would allow analysis of trends over time and the impact of economic cycles. Integrating additional variables like local employment rates, school quality, and socioeconomic indicators could provide richer insights into what drives real estate values. Detailed case studies of specific high or low-priced regions might help uncover the underlying causes of these regional disparities.

Conclusions

In summary, our analysis shows that the US housing market is complex and influenced by many factors. While property features like lot size and amenities affect prices, location and regional economic conditions are the main determinants. This study provides a snapshot of the 2023 market and lays the groundwork for future analyses. These insights highlight the importance of considering both property details and broader economic trends in real estate analysis
