House Listing Map

This map displays all property listings across the United States to explore how property prices vary by location. Each marker on the map represents a single property, and users can view detailed information like listed price, number of bedrooms and bathrooms, and square footage by clicking on each point. It reveals clear patterns in regional housing markets; major urban areas such as New York City, Los Angeles, and San Francisco show clusters of high-priced homes. In contrast, smaller cities and rural areas consist of more affordable listings. The map continues to highlight the density of listings in different areas, showing where real estate activity is most concentrated. For example, dense clusters in cities such as Los Angeles, New York, and Miami indicate high market activity, while more scattered markers in rural areas reflect lower listing volumes.

By using marker clustering, it offers better readability to understand broader trends without overwhelming the map, while still allowing close-up inspection when zoomed in. This tool is useful for buyers and sellers looking to understand price distributions across the country and compare homes by location, whether it's a luxury home surrounded by modest properties or the entire neighborhoods reflecting pricing trends.
