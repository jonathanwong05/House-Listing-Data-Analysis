Amenities Scatter Plot

This scatter plot displays the relationship between a property's total amenities (measured by the combined number of bedrooms and bathrooms) and its listed price. Each point represents an individual listing, demonstrating possible trends of how prices scale with increasing amenities. In general, there is a positive correlation: homes with more amenities tend to have higher prices. However, the relationship is not strictly linear. Some properties with the same number of amenities show wide price differences, suggesting that other factors, such as location, lot size, or interior features, could potentially play a significant roles in the listed pricings. Furthermore, this scatter plot helps in identifying clusters of typical homes (e.g., 3–4 total amenities priced between $200,000–$700,000), as well as higher-priced outliers with fewer amenities, which are likely to reflect the premium locations of the listings and the luxury design of the properties. Overall, this plot is useful for exploring and identitfying pricing patterns across the market and highlighting where buyers may be paying more for location or design rather than just size or utility.
